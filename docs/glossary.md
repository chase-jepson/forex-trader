# Forex Glossary

## Pair

A forex pair compares two currencies. `EUR/USD` means the price of one euro in US dollars. If `EUR/USD` is `1.1000`, one euro costs `1.10` US dollars.

## Pip

A pip is a common unit for measuring movement. For most `EUR/USD` prices, one pip is `0.0001`.

## Spread

Spread is the difference between the bid price and ask price. The system treats spread as a trading cost and can block trades when the spread is too wide.

## Slippage

Slippage is the difference between the expected fill price and actual fill price. It can happen during fast markets or thin liquidity.

## Liquidity

Liquidity describes how easily orders can be filled without moving the market too much. High liquidity often means tighter spreads.

## Leverage and Margin

Leverage lets a trader control a larger position with less cash. Margin is the cash reserved to support that position. Leverage can magnify losses as well as gains.

## Stop Loss

A stop loss is a required exit price that limits downside if the trade moves against the position.

## Take Profit

A take profit is an exit price that closes the trade when a target is reached.

## Position Sizing

Position sizing decides how many units to trade based on account equity, stop distance, and max risk per trade.

## Drawdown

Drawdown measures how far the account falls from a prior high.

## Expectancy

Expectancy estimates the average expected result per trade after accounting for win rate and average win/loss size.
